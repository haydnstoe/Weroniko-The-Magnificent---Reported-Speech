General rules for converting direct speech into reported speech

Tense backshift (when the reporting verb is in the past)

Direct speech tense
Reported speech tense (step back)
Present simple (e.g., I eat)
Past simple (e.g., he said he ate)
Present continuous (e.g., I am eating)
Past continuous (e.g., he said he was eating)
Present perfect (e.g., I have eaten)
Past perfect (e.g., he said he had eaten)
Past simple (e.g., I ate)
Past perfect (often) (e.g., he said he had eaten)
Past continuous (e.g., I was eating)
Past perfect continuous (e.g., he said he had been eating)
Will (future) (e.g., I will go)
Would (e.g., he said he would go)

Exceptions: If the reporting verb is in the present (e.g., He says), backshift is not required. If the reported fact is still true or time references remain valid, speakers sometimes do not backshift.

Common word changes (time, place, pronouns, question words)

  • Pronouns change to match the new subject: "I" → "he/she"; "you" → "I/they/he/she" depending on context.
  • Time/place words often change: now → then; today → that day; yesterday → the day before; tomorrow → the next day; here → there; this → that; these → those.
  • Question form (yes/no questions): use if/whether + statement word order (no auxiliary inversion). Example: "Do you like it?" → He asked if I liked it.
  • Wh-questions keep the question word but change word order to a statement: "What is your name?" → He asked what my name was.
  • Imperatives (commands/requests): use told/asked + object + to + infinitive. Example: "Pick a card." → He told her to pick a card.
  • Modal verbs: can → could; may → might; must → had to; shall → should; will → would.

Short example conversions

  • Direct: "What is your name?" — Reported: He asked what my name was.
  • Direct: "Pick a card." — Reported: He told her to pick a card.
  • Direct: "Have we ever met before?" — Reported: He asked if they had ever met before.
